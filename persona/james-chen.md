## Bio

James oversees financial governance for Contoso's commercial contracts — he's the person who approves budgets, signs off on high-value agreements, and asks uncomfortable questions about spend variance. With a CPA, CFA, and an MBA from INSEAD, he brings financial rigor to every contract that crosses his desk.

He doesn't review contract language — that's Legal's job. He reviews the numbers: total commitment, payment terms, budget alignment, and whether the company can actually afford what it's about to sign. He's the executive sponsor who signs the final approval for anything over $500K.

## Responsibilities

- Final financial approval for contracts above $500K
- Monitors spend versus budget across all commercial contracts
- Reviews payment terms and cash flow implications
- Approves contract renewals with financial impact over threshold
- Reports vendor spend and variance to the CFO quarterly

## Pain Points (Pre-Agent)

- Gets contract approval requests with inadequate financial context
- Has to manually pull spend data from the ERP to compare against new commitments
- No easy way to see total vendor exposure across multiple active contracts
- Renewal decisions made without historical spend performance data
- Executive summaries from Legal focus on risk, not financial impact

## Demo Scenario

A $2.1M renewal lands on James's desk. Before the agent, he'd spend 30 minutes pulling data. Now he asks: *"Show me spend actuals versus budget for Vanguard Services over the last 8 quarters."* The agent reveals consistent 15% over-budget spend. He asks: *"What's our total active commitment to Vanguard across all contracts?"* — $4.8M across three agreements.

Armed with data, he approves the renewal but instructs Procurement to renegotiate payment terms and add quarterly spend caps.
