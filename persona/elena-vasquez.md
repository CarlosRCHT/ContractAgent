## Bio

Elena has spent her career making sure organizations don't accidentally break the law. After earning her JD from the University of Texas and an MBA from Northwestern Kellogg, she spent a decade in Big Four consulting before joining Contoso to build their compliance function from scratch.

She oversees regulatory compliance, insurance verification, data privacy, and vendor risk — essentially, she's the last line of defense before a contract creates an obligation Contoso can't meet. Her team of five manages compliance reviews for 300+ active contracts across 14 countries.

## Responsibilities

- Oversees regulatory compliance for all vendor and commercial contracts
- Manages insurance and cybersecurity requirements verification
- Ensures data privacy regulation compliance across jurisdictions
- Maintains the corporate compliance checklist and risk scoring framework
- Reports compliance posture to the Board Risk Committee quarterly
- Coordinates with external auditors and regulators

## Pain Points (Pre-Agent)

- Compliance reviews are always the bottleneck — Procurement and Legal wait on her team
- Insurance certificate verification is manual — checking expiry dates, coverage limits, named insured
- Data privacy requirements vary by jurisdiction and contract type, hard to keep track
- No visibility into compliance trends across the portfolio ("Are we getting better or worse?")
- Incident tracking lives in spreadsheets — hard to spot patterns

## Demo Scenario

Elena's quarterly board report is due. She asks the Portfolio Intelligence Agent: *"Show me compliance incidents by severity for the last 12 months."* The agent surfaces a trend: insurance lapses have increased 40% quarter-over-quarter, concentrated in three vendors.

She drills in: *"Which active contracts with Pinnacle Systems have open compliance incidents?"* The agent returns two contracts with unresolved certificate expiry issues. Elena flags both for immediate vendor follow-up.
