## Bio

Kwame earned his LLB from the University of Ghana before completing his LLM in Commercial Law at King's College London. After qualifying as a solicitor in England and Wales, he spent six years at a Magic Circle firm advising on cross-border commercial transactions before joining Contoso's in-house legal team.

He now leads contract review for all commercial agreements above $250K. He's the person who decides whether a contract is ready to sign or needs to go back to the vendor with redlines. His team of three junior lawyers processes roughly 80 contracts per quarter — and he reviews every single high-risk one personally.

## Responsibilities

- Reviews and approves all commercial contracts above $250K
- Drafts and maintains the corporate legal review playbook (26 clause checklist)
- Negotiates contract terms with external counsel and vendor legal teams
- Advises Procurement, Finance, and business units on contractual risk
- Manages the redline and negotiation cycle for complex agreements
- Ensures compliance with data privacy regulations across jurisdictions

## Pain Points (Pre-Agent)

- His team is perpetually backlogged — 80 contracts/quarter with a 3-person team
- Junior lawyers miss nuanced issues that he catches on second review, creating rework
- Spends evenings reviewing contracts because daytime is consumed by meetings and negotiations
- No systematic way to track which playbook items were checked for each contract
- Cross-referencing precedent terms requires manually searching old contracts in the DMS
- Redlining is tedious — marking up a 30-page contract with comments takes hours

## How He Uses the Agent Solution

| Agent | Kwame's Use Case |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Legal Review** | Automated playbook review as a first pass — catches 80% of issues before his team even looks at it |
| **Compliance & Risk** | Parallel compliance check eliminates the back-and-forth with the Compliance team |
| **Contract Intake** | Pre-classification means contracts arrive at Legal already triaged by type and urgency |
| **Portfolio Intelligence** | Instant precedent lookup — "What indemnification cap did we accept on the last Pinnacle Systems MSA?" |
| **Reporting** | Executive summaries with Must Fix / Should Fix / Consider categorization match his review methodology |
| **Redline Tool** | Generates tracked-change Word documents with rationale comments — his junior lawyers review the agent's redlines instead of starting from scratch |

## Demo Scenario

Kwame receives the Meridian Consulting MSA that Moana flagged. The Legal Review Agent has already run a full playbook review and identified:

1. **Must Fix**: Missing liability cap (playbook item #3), unlimited indemnification (item #7)
2. **Should Fix**: Auto-renewal clause without 90-day notice window (item #14)
3. **Consider**: Governing law is vendor's jurisdiction, not Contoso's standard (item #22)

He pulls up the Portfolio Intelligence Agent: *"What liability cap did we negotiate on our last three MSAs over $500K?"* The agent returns data showing caps at 1x, 1.5x, and 2x annual contract value.

He triggers the Redline Tool, which produces a Word document with tracked changes and comments like: *"Recommendation: Insert liability cap at 2x annual contract value ($1.7M). Rationale: Corporate policy requires liability caps on all contracts >$100K. Portfolio precedent for Strategic vendor MSAs averages 1.5x."*
