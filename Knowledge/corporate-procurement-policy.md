## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy establishes the procurement and contract management standards for Contoso and its subsidiaries (collectively, "Contoso"). It applies to all agreements for goods, services, technology, consulting, and outsourced operations entered into by Contoso personnel, regardless of business unit or geographic location.

All employees involved in vendor selection, contract negotiation, or purchase authorization must comply with this policy. Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action, including termination.

## 2. Approval Authority & Thresholds

### 2.1 Contract Value-Based Approval Matrix

| Contract Annual Value | Approval Authority | Additional Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| < $25,000 | Department Manager | Standard PO process; no legal review required |
| $25,000 - $49,999 | Senior Manager | Procurement team notification; template contract required |
| $50,000 - $249,999 | Director | Legal review required; competitive bid (min. 3 vendors) |
| $250,000 - $499,999 | Vice-President | Legal review + compliance review; RFP required |
| $500,000 - $999,999 | Senior Vice-President | Full legal, compliance, and finance review; Board committee notification |
| $1,000,000 - $4,999,999 | Executive Vice-President / CFO | Full review suite; Board Audit & Finance Committee approval |
| >= $5,000,000 | CEO / Board of Directors | Full review suite; Board resolution required |

### 2.2 Special Approval Requirements

- **Sole-source contracts > $100,000:** Require VP approval plus written justification and Procurement Director sign-off.
- **Contracts with related parties:** Require CFO approval regardless of value.
- **Contracts involving personal data:** Require Privacy Office review regardless of value.
- **Enterprise safety-critical contracts:** Require VP Operations and VP Safety sign-off regardless of value.
- **Cross-border contracts:** Require Tax department review for any value above $50,000.

### 2.3 Amendment & Change Order Thresholds

| Change Order Value (Cumulative) | Approval Authority |
|---|---|
| < 10% of original value or < $25,000 | Original approver |
| 10-25% of original value | One level above original approver |
| > 25% of original value | Requires new contract process |

## 3. Mandatory Contract Clauses

Every Contoso contract with a value exceeding $25,000 **must** include the following clauses. Contracts using approved templates include these clauses by default. Any deviation requires Legal department approval.

### 3.1 Indemnification

The vendor must indemnify Contoso against all claims, damages, losses, and expenses arising from:
- Vendor's breach of the agreement
- Vendor's negligence or willful misconduct
- Infringement of third-party intellectual property rights
- Vendor's violation of applicable laws

**Minimum standard:** Mutual indemnification with carve-outs for IP infringement, data breach, and gross negligence/willful misconduct (vendor bears full indemnification for these).

### 3.2 Limitation of Liability

- **General cap:** The greater of (a) 2x the annual contract value, or (b) $1,000,000.
- **Uncapped carve-outs:** Indemnification for IP infringement, data breach, confidentiality breach, gross negligence, and willful misconduct.
- **Consequential damages:** Contoso's standard position is mutual exclusion of consequential damages, with carve-outs for data breach and confidentiality breach.
- **PROHIBITED:** Any clause imposing unlimited liability on Contoso, or any cap on vendor liability below 1x annual contract value.

### 3.3 Intellectual Property Ownership

- **Work product:** All deliverables created specifically for Contoso are "work made for hire" and owned by Contoso.
- **Pre-existing IP:** Vendor retains ownership but grants Contoso a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free license.
- **Joint IP:** Must be explicitly defined; default position is Contoso ownership.
- **Source code escrow:** Required for any custom software development exceeding $250,000.

### 3.4 Confidentiality

- **Term:** Obligations survive for 5 years after agreement termination (or indefinitely for trade secrets).
- **Scope:** Covers all non-public information exchanged between parties.
- **Return/destruction:** Vendor must return or certify destruction of all confidential information within 30 days of termination.
- **Permitted disclosures:** Only to vendor employees/subcontractors with need-to-know, bound by equivalent obligations.
- **Multi-language requirement:** For contracts involving customer-facing services in New York State, confidentiality provisions must address materials in all required languages.

### 3.5 Termination

- **Termination for convenience:** Contoso must retain the right to terminate for convenience with 30-90 days' written notice (based on contract value and complexity).
- **Termination for cause:** Standard 30-day cure period for material breach (except for data breach or safety incidents, which allow immediate termination).
- **Transition assistance:** Vendor must provide reasonable transition assistance for a minimum of 90 days following termination of contracts > $250,000.
- **Wind-down payments:** Only amounts for services actually rendered through termination date, plus pre-approved wind-down costs.

### 3.6 Force Majeure

- **Standard events:** Natural disasters, pandemics, government actions, war, terrorism, strikes, transportation disruptions.
- **Industry-specific events:** Infrastructure disruptions, regulatory authority directives, facility closures.
- **Notice requirement:** Affected party must provide notice within 5 business days.
- **Duration cap:** Either party may terminate if force majeure continues for more than 90 consecutive days.
- **Mitigation:** Affected party must use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate impact.

### 3.7 Data Protection & Privacy

- **Data Privacy Regulation compliance:** Required for all contracts involving personal information of domestic residents.
- **Regional Privacy Regulation:** Contracts involving local residents' personal information must comply with applicable data privacy regulations.
- **Cross-border transfers:** Personal information of domestic residents must be stored and processed domestically unless explicitly approved by the Privacy Office with appropriate safeguards.
- **Breach notification:** Vendor must notify Contoso within 24 hours of discovering a data breach.

### 3.8 Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

- **Default governing law:** Laws of the State of New York and federal laws of the domestic jurisdiction applicable therein.
- **Dispute resolution:** Negotiation -> Mediation (AAA rules) -> Arbitration (AAA rules, the designated forum) -> Litigation (the competent state court).
- **Language:** Contracts must be available in required languages where required by local jurisdiction requirements.

## 4. Prohibited Terms & Conditions

The following terms are **strictly prohibited** and must be rejected during negotiation. No exceptions without written approval from the General Counsel.

| Prohibited Term | Reason | Exception Process |
|---|---|---|
| Unlimited liability for Contoso | Unacceptable financial exposure | General Counsel only |
| Auto-renewal periods exceeding 3 years | Operational and financial lock-in risk | EVP/CFO approval required |
| Exclusive dealing arrangements | Anti-competitive; limits operational flexibility | General Counsel + CEO approval |
| Unilateral price increase rights | Budget unpredictability | Director Procurement + CFO |
| Vendor right to assign without consent | Counterparty risk | General Counsel only |
| Waiver of jury trial | Not applicable/enforceable domestically | N/A - reject outright |
| Non-compete clauses binding Contoso | Limits operational flexibility | General Counsel only |
| Automatic IP assignment to vendor | Loss of Contoso's intellectual property | General Counsel only |
| Limitation period shorter than statutory | Reduces Contoso's rights | General Counsel only |
| Mandatory foreign jurisdiction | Enforceability and cost concerns | General Counsel approval |

## 5. Payment Terms Standards

### 5.1 Standard Payment Terms by Contract Value

| Contract Annual Value | Standard Payment Terms | Exceptions |
|---|---|---|
| < $50,000 | Net 30 from invoice receipt | None |
| $50,000 - $499,999 | Net 45 from invoice receipt | Net 30 with 2% early payment discount |
| $500,000 - $4,999,999 | Net 60 from invoice receipt | Net 45 with 1.5% early payment discount |
| >= $5,000,000 | Net 90 from invoice receipt | Net 60 with 1% early payment discount |

### 5.2 Payment Conditions

- **Milestone-based payments:** Required for all project-based contracts > $250,000. No more than 25% of total value payable in advance.
- **Holdback:** 10% holdback on deliverable-based contracts until final acceptance.
- **Currency:** Payments in domestic Dollars (USD) unless foreign currency is specifically justified and approved by Treasury.
- **Late payment interest:** Standard rate is prime rate + 2%, compounding monthly.
- **Invoice requirements:** Must include PO number, detailed description of services, Sales Tax registration numbers, and supporting documentation for expenses.

### 5.3 Prohibited Payment Arrangements

- Payment terms shorter than Net 30 without VP Finance approval.
- Advance payments exceeding 25% of total contract value.
- Payment in cryptocurrency or non-standard instruments.
- Payment to jurisdictions on FATF grey/black lists without enhanced due diligence.

### 6.1 Requirements by Contract Type

| Contract Type | Commercial General Liability | Professional Liability (E&O) | Cyber/Tech E&O | Workers' Compensation | Automobile |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| General Services | $2M per occurrence / $5M aggregate | - | - | Statutory | $2M (if applicable) |
| Professional/Consulting | $2M per occurrence / $5M aggregate | $5M per claim / $10M aggregate | - | Statutory | $2M (if applicable) |
| Technology/SaaS | $2M per occurrence / $5M aggregate | $5M per claim / $10M aggregate | $10M per claim | Statutory | - |
| Construction/Facilities | $5M per occurrence / $10M aggregate | $5M per claim | - | Statutory | $5M |
| Enterprise Services (On-site) | $10M per occurrence / $25M aggregate | $10M per claim | - | Statutory | $5M |
| Data Processing | $2M per occurrence / $5M aggregate | $5M per claim | $15M per claim | Statutory | - |

### 6.2 General Insurance Requirements

- Contoso must be named as an additional insured on all CGL policies.
- Policies must provide 30 days' written notice prior to cancellation or material change.
- Certificates of insurance must be provided prior to contract commencement and annually thereafter.
- Vendors must maintain insurance for the duration of the contract plus 2 years for claims-made policies.
- Deductibles exceeding $100,000 require Procurement Director approval.
- Insurers must be rated A- (Excellent) or better by AM Best.

## 7. Cybersecurity Requirements for Technology Vendors

### 7.1 Mandatory Security Standards

All technology vendors with access to Contoso systems or data must comply with:

- **SOC 2 Type II certification** (or equivalent) - current report required before contract execution.
- **ISO 27001 certification** - required for vendors processing or storing Contoso data.
- **Data residency:** All Contoso data must be stored within the domestic jurisdiction unless explicitly approved by CISO. Processing may occur in the US with appropriate safeguards.
- **Encryption:** AES-256 at rest; TLS 1.2+ in transit. No exceptions.
- **Access controls:** Multi-factor authentication (MFA) required for all access to Contoso systems or data.
- **Vulnerability management:** Vendors must remediate critical vulnerabilities within 72 hours and high-severity within 30 days.
- **Penetration testing:** Annual third-party penetration test required; results shared with Contoso.
- **Incident response:** Vendor must notify Contoso within 24 hours of any security incident. Full incident report within 72 hours.

### 7.2 Data Classification & Handling

| Data Classification | Storage Requirements | Access Requirements | Breach Notification |
|---|---|---|---|
| Public | Standard | Standard | 72 hours |
| Internal | Encrypted at rest | Role-based access | 48 hours |
| Confidential | Encrypted, domestic-only | MFA + role-based + audit logging | 24 hours |
| Restricted (PII, Financial) | Encrypted, domestic-only, isolated | MFA + privileged access management + real-time monitoring | Immediate (within 4 hours) |

### 7.3 Right to Audit

Contoso retains the right to:
- Conduct security assessments or audits with 30 days' notice.
- Require remediation of identified deficiencies within agreed timelines.
- Terminate the agreement immediately in the event of a material security breach.

## 8. Tax Compliance Requirements

### 8.1 domestic Tax Requirements

- Vendors must provide valid Sales Tax registration numbers and Sales Tax registration (for locally-based services).
- Invoices must comply with the tax authority requirements for input tax credits.
- Non-resident vendors must comply with withholding tax requirements under the applicable tax laws.
- Vendors performing services in multiple states must properly allocate and charge applicable state/regional taxes.

### 8.2 Cross-Border Considerations

- Contracts with US vendors exceeding $50,000 require Tax department review for treaty benefit application.
- International contracts must address permanent establishment risk.
- Transfer pricing documentation required for intercompany agreements.
- Withholding tax obligations must be clearly assigned (vendor responsibility to claim treaty benefits).
- Vendors must provide applicable tax forms (e.g., W-8BEN-E for US treaty benefits).

### 8.3 Enterprise-Specific Tax Considerations

- Service surcharges and industry-specific levies must be separately identified.
- Ground handling services may have different tax treatment by jurisdiction.
- International region-specific services must consider bilateral service agreement implications.

## 9. Standard Review SLAs

### 9.1 Contract Review Turnaround Times

| Contract Type | Value Range | Standard Review SLA | Expedited Review SLA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard services (template) | < $100,000 | 5 business days | 2 business days |
| Standard services (custom) | < $100,000 | 10 business days | 5 business days |
| Complex services | $100,000 - $500,000 | 15 business days | 7 business days |
| Strategic/high-value | > $500,000 | 20 business days | 10 business days |
| Amendments/renewals | Any | 5 business days | 2 business days |
| NDAs (template) | Any | 3 business days | 1 business day |
| NDAs (custom) | Any | 7 business days | 3 business days |

### 9.2 Escalation for Delayed Reviews

| Delay | Escalation Level |
|---|---|
| 2 business days past SLA | Procurement Director |
| 5 business days past SLA | VP Procurement |
| 10 business days past SLA | General Counsel / EVP |

### 9.3 Expedited Review Criteria

Expedited review may be requested when:
- Business-critical deadline within 10 business days.
- Safety or regulatory compliance requirement.
- Board or executive directive.
- Revenue-generating opportunity at risk.

Requests must be approved by the requesting VP and acknowledged by the Legal department.

## 10. Vendor Classification & Due Diligence

### 10.1 Vendor Risk Tiers

| Tier | Criteria | Due Diligence Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 - Critical | Annual spend > $1M or operational dependency | Full due diligence, annual review, business continuity plan required |
| Tier 2 - Significant | Annual spend $250K-$1M | Standard due diligence, biennial review |
| Tier 3 - Standard | Annual spend $50K-$250K | Basic due diligence, triennial review |
| Tier 4 - Low | Annual spend < $50K | Simplified due diligence at onboarding |

### 10.2 Due Diligence Checklist

- Financial statements (last 3 years) - Tier 1 and 2
- Credit report - All tiers
- References (minimum 3) - Tier 1, 2, and 3
- Sanctions/denied parties screening - All tiers
- Beneficial ownership verification - Tier 1 and 2
- Insurance certificates - All tiers (per Section 6)
- Security certifications - Technology vendors (per Section 7)
- ESG/sustainability assessment - Tier 1

## 11. Contract Lifecycle Management

### 11.1 Key Milestones

| Milestone | Timeline | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| Contract expiry notification | 180 days before expiry | Procurement |
| Renewal assessment | 150 days before expiry | Business owner + Procurement |
| Renewal negotiation | 120 days before expiry | Procurement + Legal |
| Renewal execution | 60 days before expiry | All approvers per Section 2 |
| Post-execution filing | Within 5 business days | Procurement (Document Management System/SharePoint) |

### 11.2 Record Retention

- Executed contracts: 7 years after termination.
- Supporting documents (RFPs, evaluations): 5 years after contract termination.
- Financial records: Per tax authority requirements (minimum 6 years).

## 12. Policy Exceptions

Any exception to this policy must be:
1. Requested in writing with business justification.
2. Reviewed by the Procurement Director.
3. Approved by the appropriate authority per the matrix below.
4. Documented in the contract file with the exception approval attached.

| Exception Type | Approval Authority |
|---|---|
| Approval threshold deviation | One level above standard approver |
| Missing mandatory clause | General Counsel |
| Prohibited term acceptance | General Counsel + CFO |
| Payment terms deviation | VP Finance |
| Insurance requirement reduction | VP Risk Management |
| Cybersecurity requirement waiver | CISO + General Counsel |
